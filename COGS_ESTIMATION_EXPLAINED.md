## 📊 **Current Calculation (ESTIMATE)**

### **What We're Assuming:**

```
COGS (Cost of Goods Sold) = 25% of Sale Price
```

**For your product:**
```
Sale Price: 110 EGP
COGS (ESTIMATED): 110 × 0.25 = 27.50 EGP ⚠️ ASSUMPTION!
```

### **Why 25%?**

This is a **common industry benchmark** for retail products:
- Retail products typically aim for **3-4x markup**
- If you sell at 110 EGP, manufacturer cost is often ~25-35 EGP
- **But this varies WIDELY by product type!**

## ✅ **Solution: You MUST Know Your Real Cost!**

### **How to Find Manufacturer Price:**

**1. Contact Suppliers:**
- Alibaba.com (Chinese manufacturers)
- AliExpress (wholesale)
- Local distributors

**2. Ask for MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity) pricing:**
```
"What's your price for:
- 100 units?
- 500 units?
- 1,000 units?"
```

**3. Factor in ALL costs:**
- Manufacturing price
- Shipping to your location
- Import duties/taxes
- Quality inspection
- Packaging

**Real COGS = Manufacturing + Shipping + Duties + Other**

## 🎯 **What You Should Do:**

### **Step 1: Find Real Manufacturer Price**
Contact suppliers and get quotes including shipping

### **Step 2: Calculate Break-Even Price**

```
Minimum Price = COGS + Fees
              = 100 + 47
              = 147 EGP (break-even)

Target Price = COGS + Fees + Desired Profit (30%)
             = 100 + 47 + 44
             = 191 EGP (for 30% margin)
```

### **Step 3: Check Competitiveness**

If competitors sell at **110 EGP** but you need **191 EGP** to profit:
- ❌ **You can't compete!**
- 🔍 Find cheaper supplier
- 🔍 Find different product
